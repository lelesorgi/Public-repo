Comments: The three distributions are always centred in the same location for the three models indeed the means are all equal to the sample mean. The differences are in the covariances matrices of the distribution. For LDA we assume that \(\Sigma\) is the same for each class, so that the three distributions have the same shape, even though they are located in different regions. As regard as QDA the distributions of the predictors given the species,are the most different. Indeed each covariance matrix is estimated with the sample covariance, so that the ellipsis are diverse, both in shape and size. Lastly, with Gaussian Naive Bayes, supposing that the variables are independent, we obtain distributions with the same circular shape but with different ranges which are determined by the different variances in the diagonals.

All the classifiers recorded extremely high values in the evaluation. Nonetheless we can make some comments about the slightly differences. QDA and Naive Bayes record an higher accuracy. This is not surprising as the covariances of the predictors vary a lot between the classes, so that the LDA hypothesis seems not to stand. We would expect QDA to be the best model. Indeed it is the most flexible model and the risk of overfitting, is lowered by the small number of predictors, which decrease the variance of the learner. Besides, Naive Bayes assumption of independence between the feature seems not to be respectedn as they are extremely correlated.
